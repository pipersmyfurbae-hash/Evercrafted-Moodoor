How matching works

From a few sentences to the wreath that holds them

No quiz, no style survey, no scrolling through sixty options. You write a memory; the system does the rest. Here's exactly how — including the parts most companies wouldn't explain.

01

You write the memory

A porch, a person, a season, a year. Write it the way you'd tell a friend — a sentence is enough, a paragraph is better. There are no wrong answers and no design vocabulary required.

"Sunday mornings at my grandmother's lake house, the summer before we sold it."
02

The feeling gets read

Our Emotional Vector System scores the memory on seven dimensions — how warm it is, how quiet, how time-soaked, how close to the chest. This is the one step where AI is involved, and it does exactly one job: interpret the feeling.

warmth .79 · nostalgia .94 · intimacy .81 · valence .62 · restraint .55 · energy .33 · seasonal .71
Honest note: the AI never chooses your wreath. It only translates words into the seven numbers above.
03

The library is ranked — by math, not by model

Every design in the library was profiled on the same seven dimensions when it was curated. Your memory's profile is compared against all sixty using a fixed, published formula. The same memory always produces the same matches — nothing is random, nothing is "personalized" behind your back.

04

The reveal

You see your top matches, each with its story, its emotional profile, and the choice that makes Moodoor different: order it finished from a small numbered run, or download the exact blueprint and build it yourself.

The seven dimensions

What the system actually listens for

Each dimension runs from 0 to 1. Together they form a memory's emotional fingerprint.

Warmth emotional temperature

The tenderness in the memory — its glow.

0 — clinical, cold1 — a kitchen in winter

Energy arousal level

Whether the memory sits still or moves.

0 — perfect stillness1 — a full house, music on

Nostalgia pull toward the past

How time-soaked the memory is. The strongest signal in most memories people share.

0 — entirely present-tense1 — aching for then

Valence positivity

The brightness of the feeling. Grief can run high on nostalgia and low here — the system keeps them separate on purpose.

0 — grief, loss1 — uncomplicated joy

Intimacy closeness of the circle

Whether the memory belongs to a crowd or to two people on a porch.

0 — public, anonymous1 — between two, or one

Restraint quietness

How composed the feeling is. Not the opposite of energy — a wedding toast can be loud and held at once.

0 — loud, exuberant1 — hushed, composed

Seasonal affinity anchoring to a season

How inseparable the memory is from its time of year — Octobers you can smell, Decembers with a temperature.

0 — could be any month1 — is its season
What happens to your memory

You're trusting us with something personal. Here's our end of it.

It's used for matching, full stop

Your memory and its profile are stored so we can show you your matches, improve match quality, and let you return to your reveal. It is never published, never shown to other customers, never sold.

It trains nothing

Your words aren't used to train AI models — ours or anyone else's. The interpreter reads your memory once, scores it, and that's the end of its involvement.

You can ask us to forget it

One email and the memory, its profile, and its session are deleted. The wreath, if you bought one, is yours to keep regardless.

The match math is inspectable

The ranking formula is fixed and versioned. If you ever want to know why a design matched, we can show you the arithmetic — dimension by dimension.
