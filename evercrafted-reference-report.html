Design Laws Reference

Odd Counts · Always

Every individual floral species count must be odd: 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9. Never 2, 4, 6, or 8. Applies to every product, every species, every layer. Non-negotiable.

Bow Rule

All products get a bow EXCEPT: Candle Rings (R6), Window Wreath (R4), Shelf Drape (A4), Bottle Brush Trees (S4), Hoop Wreath (S6 gets a ribbon tail only). Large droopy looped layered bows — never stiff, never symmetrical.

Multi-Ribbon System

Up to 3 ribbons of different widths. Statement (widest, solid), Character (mid-width, pattern), Accent (narrowest, texture/sheen). Never two patterns together. Describe all widths, materials, colors, and patterns explicitly.

Floral Hierarchy

Tier 1 (hero) at Layer 1 only. Tier 2 (supporting) at Layers 2–3. Tier 3 (discovery) at Layer 4 only. Tier 1 is completely absent from discovery products.

Color Movement

Full saturation at Entry → Midtone at Anchor rooms → Whisper tone at Discovery. The palette gets quieter as the eye moves deeper into the home. Neutral anchor (greenery) constant at all layers.

Greenery-Only Products

D1 Puddle Post, A5/A6 Kitchen Garlands, A4 Shelf Drape, R2 Cabinet Wreaths, R4 Window Wreath, R7 Lantern Collar carry zero florals. Greenery, texture, and ribbon do all the work.

Species Diversity

Minimum 7 distinct botanical species across the full collection. Layer 1: 5 species. Layers 2-3: 4 species. Layer 4: 3 species. No two products share all the same species.

PIE R1-R18

Wreath products follow the Placement Intelligence Engine. Classic coverage 33–55% of ring. Asymmetry score 0.40–0.80. Drama pole at t=0.7 of arc. Silence arc ≥120° opposition from mass centroid.

Sightline Architecture

Every collection is built as a sightline story. Products are selected based on which home spaces are relevant, not from a fixed list. Minimum: one hero wreath + one Layer 2 draw product + one Layer 3 anchor + one Layer 4 discovery.

The HeyGen Proposal System

The client proposal is not a document. It's a private, beautifully designed web experience sent as a single link. The client taps it on their phone and gets a page that feels like it was made specifically for them — because it was. Your avatar appears three times throughout the experience, each time with a different role.

The Proposal Page — What the Client Experiences

1
The Cover Their name in 82px elegant typography on a dark editorial page. Collection name, season, palette chips. Three seconds of silence. Then your avatar appears.
2
Designer Introduction (90 seconds) Your avatar in an editorial room setting — dark wall, warm side light. Not a pitch. A statement of design philosophy built around the sightline concept applied to their specific home.
3
The Sightline Story Three full-width scroll panels: Layer 1 (what you see at the door), Layer 2 (what pulls you deeper), Layer 3 (where it resolves). Lifestyle render photography in each panel.
4
The Home Blueprint An interactive SVG floor plan showing every botanical placement in the home. Clicking any dot scrolls to that product's card. Your avatar's 10-second floor plan voiceover plays here.
5
The Collection Products in sightline order. Pencil illustration sketch shows by default → hover dissolves to full color render. Product name, placement description, species details, price beneath each.
6
Investment Summary Clean table organized by sightline layer. Total estimate. Professional but not clinical.
7
The Close (20 seconds) Your avatar returns. Brief, warm, personal. Then a single button: "Begin Your Installation" linking to your Calendly.

The Variable System

Record one master script per collection. HeyGen's variable text system inserts the client name and one personalized home detail automatically. One recording — infinite personalizations. Each proposal feels entirely bespoke without re-recording.

Variable format: [CLIENT_NAME] · [COLLECTION_NAME] · [HOME_DETAIL] — e.g. "your staircase and the living room beyond it"

Workflow — Intake to Sent Link

1
Client Intake Home style, rooms to treat, season, budget range. 10-minute conversation or form.
2
Collection Selection Choose or generate the right collection. The engine selects products based on the home profile.
3
Proposal Generation Paste the collection JSON into the proposal generator. Client name in. Full page built automatically.
4
Send the Link One URL. One text message. Done. Total time from intake to sent link: under one hour.

The Three Avatar Scripts

Script 01 · The Introduction · 85–90 seconds · ~215 words
"[CLIENT_NAME].

Thank you for being here.

I want to start by telling you something I tell every client before I show them anything. When I walk into a home for the first time, I always go back to the front door. I open it. I stand in the threshold. And I ask myself one question.

What do you see the moment it opens?

Not what's decorated. Not what's been styled. What does the eye travel to — instinctively, before a single conscious thought?

That question is where everything I do begins. Because when a home is designed botanically from the inside out — when every placement exists in conversation with every other placement — that first sightline moment becomes something you feel before you understand it.

What I've built for you in this proposal is an answer to that question. For your home. For this season.

Every placement you're about to see was chosen for what it does to the eye as it moves through your space. Not just what it looks like on a wall.

Scroll through slowly.

I'll see you at the end."
Deliver slowly. The sightline question is the thesis — slow down there. "Scroll through slowly" is an invitation, not a directive.
Recording setup: Darkest wall you have as background. One light source from left or right — not in front of you. Wall behind you reads as shadow. This puts you inside the Evercrafted world, not in front of it.
Script 02 · The Floor Plan Moment · 10 seconds · ~28 words
"Every dot on this plan is a decision I made about your home.

Tap any one of them."
Delivered as the floor plan section scrolls into view — audio only, no video. The voice arrives while the client is looking at the plan. Say nothing more.
Option A (recommended): Audio file only — embeds as ambient voice that plays when the blueprint section enters the viewport. Client stays focused on the plan while hearing you. Most unexpected and elegant.

Option B: Small floating HeyGen video in lower-left corner. Appears for 10 seconds, fades out. Plan stays interactive behind it.
Script 03 · The Close · 18–22 seconds · ~50 words
"Now you've seen how I see your home.

The only thing left is to begin.

When you're ready — and there's no rush — tap the button below. Let's talk through it together.

I'd love to bring this to life for you."
First sentence almost to yourself. Long pause after it. "There's no rush" — mean it. End warmly, not professionally.

Build Phase Plan

The order of operations to make the full proposal system live and revenue-generating.

01

Avatar Recording

Write the three scripts into HeyGen. Record Script 01 (Introduction) and Script 03 (Close) as video. Record Script 02 (Floor Plan) as audio only. Film in your darkest room with side lighting. The whole collection is Quiet Accord — record that version first. One session, three clips.

02

Render the Collection

Run the Quiet Accord phase-stack prompts for all 11 products. For each product: Phase 1 (greenery base) → Phase 2 (hero florals) → Phase 3 (accents + ribbon). Then run the Sketch prompt for each product. Paste all URLs into the Collection Studio app. Export the collection JSON with all image URLs embedded.

03

First Proposal

Take the completed Quiet Accord collection JSON and your HeyGen embed URLs. Open the proposal HTML file. Fill in the PROPOSAL configuration block at the top (client name, HeyGen URLs, Calendly link, image URLs). Drop the file on Netlify Drop — instant URL. Send to your first client.

04

The Proposal Generator

Build a simple web tool where you type the client name and paste the collection JSON → it builds and outputs the full proposal page link automatically. No more manual HTML editing. 15 seconds from collection to client link. This is the phase that makes the system scalable.
