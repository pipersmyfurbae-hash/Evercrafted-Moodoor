The design story

How a memory becomes a wreath

September Porch began with a memory submitted during the EVS calibration period — seven sentences about a grandmother's screened porch, the particular smell of cedar and cooling coffee, and a door that rattled in the frame when anyone walked past. The feeling it scored for: high nostalgia, high seasonal affinity, moderate warmth, low energy. A memory inseparable from its month.

The design reflects it literally: the asymmetric amber-and-cedar composition, the low center of gravity, the exposed grapevine running open between 1:00 and 3:30 — space that holds as much meaning as the stems that fill it.
